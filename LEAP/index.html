LEAP

Learning Articulated Occupancy of People

LEAP is a neural network architecture for representing volumetric animatable human bodies.

Inspired by traditional human body models techniques, LEAP models human bodies in the canonical pose and is controlled through carefully designed latent codes that encode shape and pose dependent deformations. The whole model is end-to-end differentiable and compatible with optimization and deep learning pipelines.





Overview

We introduce a novel deep neural network which, given a set of bone transformations (joint locations and rotations) and a query point in space, first maps the query point to a canonical space via learned backward linear blend skinning (LBS) weights and then efficiently queries the occupancy value via an occupancy network that models accurate identity and pose dependent deformations in the canonical space.

To account for the undefined skinning weights for the points that are not on the surface of a human body, LEAP leverages the consistency relationship between the forward LBS and the backward LBS weights and incorporates cycle-distance based features into the occupancy network.

LEAP captures accurate identify-dependent and pose-dependent deformations through advanced encoding schemes that incorporate prior knowledge about the kinematic structure and plausible shape of a human body.






We release a pretrained LEAP that generalizes to unseen human bodies and poses and which could be easily integrated into learning pipelines that require an efficient occupancy check to resolve collisions with other objects or environment.
